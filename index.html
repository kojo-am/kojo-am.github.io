Education, Data Science, and Inclusive Practice

A portfolio of writing and projects exploring how data, professional judgment, and responsible technology can support better decisions in education.

Written by Emmanuel Adu Amoako, this website brings together education writing, public-facing commentary, and developing data science work. Its focus is careful interpretation, inclusive practice, and the responsible use of evidence in schools.

Featured Series: Interpreting School Data More Carefully

A four-part public-facing series exploring what school assessment data in England can reveal, what it can hide, and why careful interpretation matters.

  • Article 1: What school assessment data can show and what it cannot An introduction to the limits of reading assessment data as self-explanatory, using Newham and England as an illustrative example.
  • Article 2: Can Data Science Support Earlier Identification of Learning Needs in Primary Classrooms? Exploring whether data science can help schools notice emerging patterns of need earlier, and why its value depends on interpretation, ethics, and professional oversight.
  • Article 3: Why Teacher Professional Judgement Still Matters in Data-Informed Education Coming soon — examining why context, interpretation and classroom knowledge remain essential.
  • Article 4: What Ethical Education Technology Should Look Like in Primary Schools Coming soon — considering inclusion, fairness and responsible design in primary education technology.

About the Site

This site is designed as a growing portfolio. It combines a structured article series on the interpretation of school data, broader commentary on assessment and inclusion, and future data science projects focused on educational questions.

Across all sections, the central concern remains the same: how evidence should be interpreted carefully, how professional judgment still matters, and how data-informed systems can be designed more responsibly.

About the Author

Emmanuel Adu Amoako is a data scientist and educator interested in how data, predictive analysis, and intelligent systems can be used responsibly to support better decisions in education. His work focuses on careful interpretation, inclusive practice, practical application, and the ethical use of data-driven tools in schools.
