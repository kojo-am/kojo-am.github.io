Education, Evidence
and Inclusive Practice

Writing and projects at the intersection of classroom experience, data science, and the honest interpretation of evidence in schools.

Kojo Amoako is a teacher and data scientist with an interest in how evidence, professional judgement, and responsible technology can support better decisions in education. This site brings together public-facing education writing, commentary on assessment and inclusion, and developing data science work. The concern running through all of it is the same: what does the evidence actually say, and what does it miss?

Featured Series: Interpreting School Data More Carefully

A four-part public-facing series exploring what school assessment data in England can reveal, what it can hide, and why careful interpretation matters.

  • Article 1 — Published What School Assessment Data Can Show — and What It Cannot An introduction to the limits of reading assessment data as self-explanatory, using Newham and England as an illustrative example.
  • Article 2 — Published Can Data Science Support Earlier Identification of Learning Needs in Primary Classrooms? Exploring whether data science can help schools notice emerging patterns of need earlier, and why its value depends on interpretation, ethics, and professional oversight.
  • Article 3 — Coming Soon Why Teacher Professional Judgement Still Matters in Data-Informed Education Examining why context, interpretation, and classroom knowledge remain essential alongside any data system.
  • Article 4 — Coming Soon What Ethical Education Technology Should Look Like in Primary Schools Considering inclusion, fairness, and responsible design in primary education technology.

Education Commentary

Short essays on assessment, inclusion, curriculum, and the realities of school life.

  • Essay — Published What SATs Scores Miss On the pressure KS2 SATs place on children, the narrowing of the curriculum, and why a test that does not determine secondary school admission still shapes what happens when children arrive there.
  • Essay — Published When Inclusion Becomes Paperwork On SEN, curriculum access, pupil-led learning, and what meaningful education can look like for children with complex needs when we are willing to follow their lead.

About This Site

This site is designed as a growing portfolio. It combines a structured article series on the interpretation of school data, broader commentary on assessment and inclusion, and future data science projects focused on educational questions.

Across all sections, the central concern is the same: how evidence should be interpreted carefully, how professional judgement still matters, and how data-informed systems can be designed more responsibly for the children they affect.

About the Author

Teacher

A primary school teacher with particular interests in inclusion, special educational needs, outdoor learning, and assessment that serves children rather than just measuring them.

Data Scientist

Working at the intersection of data science and education, with a focus on responsible use of evidence, early identification of learning needs, and the ethical design of data-informed systems in schools.
